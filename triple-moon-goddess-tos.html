IIBirth Data & Research Data Collection

Birth Data Requirement

All TMG tiers require birth data (date, time, and location of birth) to generate a natal chart. Birth data is used solely to calculate your astrological chart and derive the platform outputs associated with your account.

Research Data Collection

By using the Platform, you agree that your session data contributes to the TMG longitudinal research corpus. This is a core function of the Platform. The research purpose is to evaluate whether astrological aspects — derived from your exact birth data — correlate with behavioral and psychological patterns over time. Birth data is therefore present in the research corpus by necessity: it is the variable being studied.

Research records are pseudonymized: your birth data is stored under a code and is never linked to your name or email address in research records. Data is analyzed at the pattern level only. Either outcome of the research — correlation or its absence — contributes to the methodology.

Pseudonymization

Birth data (date, time, and location) is inherently identifying and cannot be truly anonymized. Pseudonymization is the applicable standard: your identity (name, email) is replaced with a code, stored separately from research records, and the link is protected. Your birth data appears in the research corpus under that code — not under your name — and is analyzed at the pattern level only.

IIIPlatform Outputs

Non-Diagnostic by Design

All TMG outputs are probabilistic assessments of constitutional patterns and personal growth likelihoods. They are not diagnoses, clinical assessments, or treatment recommendations. The platform is non-diagnostic as a methodological principle — not merely a research-validation gap.

IPA Gap Analysis

The IPA (Integrative Pattern Analysis) Gap Analysis methodology is patent-pending (U.S. Provisional Patent #63/998,305). IPA scores are collected and stored as research hypothesis variables. They are not surfaced in platform outputs at this stage and are not used as predictors until sufficient research data has been collected and validated.
