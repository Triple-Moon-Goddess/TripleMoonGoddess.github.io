IIBirth Data & Research Data Collection

Birth Data Is Collected Only From Individual Seekers

Birth data (date, time, and location of birth) is collected only at the Individual Seeker (free) tier, and is entered by the seeker themselves into their own account. Birth data is used solely to calculate the seeker's natal chart and derive the platform outputs associated with their account.

Practitioners Do Not See Client Birth Data

Practitioner accounts on the Integrative Practitioner, Soul Pattern, and Constitutional Health tiers do not collect or display client birth data. When a practitioner views a client's record, the practitioner sees the chart-derived outputs — sign placements, house positions, constitutional profile, pattern scores — but not the underlying birth date, time, or location. The only client identifier visible to the practitioner is the client's email address, which the practitioner has entered themselves and which is encrypted at rest in the practitioner's account.

Sign and house information cannot be reverse-engineered into the identifying birth data the chart was built from. The chart describes the person; it does not identify them.

Research Data Collection

By using the Platform, you agree that your session data contributes to the TMG longitudinal research corpus. This is a core function of the Platform. The TMG research corpus is astrological research — it studies the relationship between natal chart patterns and self-reported lived experience, across both personal growth patterns (Soul Module) and constitutional health patterns (Health Module). It is not clinical research and does not produce clinical findings.

Research records are pseudonymized: birth data is stored under a code and is never linked to a name or email address in research records. Data is analyzed at the pattern level only. Either outcome of the research — correlation or its absence — contributes to the methodology.

Practitioner Research Participation

Practitioners on paid tiers contribute an additional layer of research data: the practitioner's own astrological observations, pattern scores, and longitudinal follow-up feedback entered through the platform. This practitioner-level data is the highest-confidence layer of the TMG research corpus — it pairs astrological prediction with professional observation over time.

By activating a paid practitioner account, you consent to this research participation. Your contributions are pseudonymized and stored at the pattern level only. You do not own or hold rights to the research corpus or any aggregated outputs derived from it. Your individual account data remains yours; the pseudonymized research record does not.

Pseudonymization

Birth data (date, time, and location) is inherently identifying and cannot be truly anonymized. Pseudonymization is the applicable standard: identity (name, email) is replaced with a code, stored separately from research records, and the link is protected. Birth data appears in the research corpus under that code — not under a name — and is analyzed at the pattern level only.
