The Problem I'm Adding To

Therapy is extraordinary. It has saved lives — including mine. The clinical tools practitioners use every day have helped millions of people and represent decades of accumulated wisdom. IPA is not here to replace any of that. It is here to add to it — to make existing clinical work more efficient and more comprehensive by providing a layer of context that currently doesn't exist.

That missing layer is the ability to distinguish between who a person was born as, who life shaped them to become, and where they exercised agency in between — as three separate, simultaneously readable dimensions.

This distinction matters most for a specific population — one that is chronically underserved by existing clinical tools:

People carrying umbrella diagnoses like fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue syndrome, and autoimmune conditions — real symptoms, inadequate explanations.

People with PTSD and complex trauma whose current presentation masks their constitutional baseline.

People who have been told their symptoms are exaggerated, psychosomatic, or not real.

People who have been misdiagnosed, underdiagnosed, or dismissed — told they are crazy or exaggerating when they are neither.
For this population especially, knowing the difference between a constitutional pattern and an adaptive one is not just clinically useful — it is validating in a way that can change the entire treatment relationship. It tells the person: this is real, this is yours, and here is why.

The Zygote and the Journey

Here is the simplest way I know to explain what IPA does:

The zygote is who you were at conception — before environment, before experience, before anyone got to you. Complete. Encoded. Fully yourself before the world had a chance to respond to you.

The journey is everything that happened after — the family you were born into, the experiences that shaped you, the adaptations you made to survive and belong and be loved.

The gap between the zygote and the journey is where you exercised free will — consciously or not. It is the most clinically rich territory in any assessment. It is where change is possible. It is where the work lives.

IPA makes all three readable simultaneously — in a single individual, from a single assessment.

Nature, Nurture, and Free Will

The nature vs. nurture debate has been going on for centuries. Researchers, philosophers, and clinicians have argued about which one determines who we become.

IPA's answer is simple: it's both. It has always been both. And the space between them is where free will actually lives.

While existing frameworks acknowledge that both nature and nurture matter, no tool has been able to map both as simultaneously readable layers in a single person — with the gap between them identified as clinically useful information. IPA adds this dimension to the existing clinical conversation. Not replacing what already works. Adding what has been missing.

What the System Actually Does

IPA is both a methodology (a way of thinking) and an engine (a software tool that does the calculation). They work together — one doesn't function without the other.

Layer 1 — The Blueprint (Nature)

Your constitutional predisposition. Who you were born as. The innate patterns encoded before experience. Think of it as your factory settings — not good or bad, just yours. Scored across four ancient constitutional frameworks simultaneously: Greek humoral theory, Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and traditional medical astrology.

Layer 2 — The Adaptation (Nurture)

Your developmental expression. Who life shaped you to become. Where your innate patterns found expression — or couldn't. Where you adapted, compensated, redirected. Drawing on the tradition of psychological and modern pattern assessment.

Layer 3 — The Gap (Free Will)

Where the Blueprint and the Adaptation diverge. The precise points where your innate nature had to negotiate with your developmental experience. Where you made choices — conscious or not. This is the most clinically rich layer. This is where the work lives. This is where change is possible.

Four Ancient Traditions. One Unified Tool.

IPA brings four classical systems of understanding human constitution into a single unified conversation. Each tradition has been developed and refined over centuries. Each contains genuine clinical wisdom. None of them is diminished by being brought alongside the others — each is honored as essential.

Greek Humoral Theory

One of the oldest Western medical systems, mapping four constitutional types based on elemental qualities: Hot/Dry (Choleric), Hot/Moist (Sanguine), Cold/Moist (Phlegmatic), Cold/Dry (Melancholic). The foundational architecture of IPA — the simplest, most universal constitutional logic.

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)

Thousands of years of thermal and moisture theory, organ system patterns, and constitutional assessment. Provides rich clinical language for physical and energetic patterns.

Ayurveda

One of the world's oldest holistic healing systems. Three constitutional types (Vata, Pitta, Kapha) that map beautifully alongside Greek humoral theory, with practical guidance on herbs, diet, and lifestyle.

Medical/Traditional Astrology & Psychological/Modern Astrology

Two astrological traditions that together provide the calculation engine. Both are honored as essential. Neither is privileged over the other.
